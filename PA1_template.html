Imputing missing values

  1. The total number of missing values in the dataset is
length(which(is.na(data$steps)))
## [1] 2304
  1. Imputing data, here we will fill NAs with the mean for the respective 5-min intervals.
  2. The new data set is named impData
aSpInt = DT[ , mean(steps, na.rm=T), by=interval]; impData = data
for (i in 1:nrow(impData)) { 
  if (is.na(impData$steps[i])) {
      impData$steps[i] = aSpInt$V1[which(aSpInt$interval == impData$interval[i])]
    } 
  }

  1. The histogram (of steps per day) now has peak at the mean of total number of steps taken per day. The mean is unchanged, and median is now equal to the mean (though the change is unsignificant). This is because (through quick visual inspection) NAs were not random, they occurred in each whole day (i.e., if a date contains an interval with NA, then all of its intervals are NA). By imputing data with means of interval across all days in the dataset, we are effectively filling these NA dates with the average number of steps taken per day (sum of averages = average of sums). We added over 86129 steps over 8 NA dates.
